Cookie Guide

Cookies are small files that websites store on your device to remember information about your visit. They help the website function smoothly and make your experience better. Here’s a simple guide:

Types of Cookies

  • Necessary Cookies: Essential for basic functions like logging in and saving your settings. Without these, the website may not work properly.
  • Analytics Cookies: Help website owners see how visitors use the site. This data is used to improve the site’s content and layout over time.
  • Functional Cookies: Allow extra features, like enabling comments or sharing content on social media, to make your experience more enjoyable.
  • Performance Cookies: Help monitor and improve website speed and responsiveness, making browsing smoother.
  • Advertisement Cookies: These cookies allow websites to show ads that are relevant to you, based on your browsing habits.
  • HttpOnly Cookies: Special cookies that protect sensitive information. They can only be read by the website’s server, not by your browser, which helps keep your data more secure.
  • Other Cookies: These are cookies that don’t fall into the above categories and may be for unique functions or future analysis.

Managing Cookies

You can control or limit cookies in your browser’s settings:

  • Google Chrome: Go to Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and Other Site Data.
  • Firefox: Go to Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data.
  • Safari: Go to Preferences > Privacy to adjust settings.

Why Cookies Matter

Cookies make using websites easier by saving your preferences and settings. Knowing about cookies helps you manage your privacy and security online.
